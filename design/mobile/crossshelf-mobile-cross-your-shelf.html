What this page does

  • Reached by tapping the "Cross your shelf" section header from For You, or via a dedicated card on Explore.
  • Italic page title reinforces the platform's signature feature — same italic Playfair treatment used for the section in For You.
  • Intro line ("All the connections between what you love") gives editorial voice. Subline gives the actual numbers — "47 connections from 9 items you rated highly." Anchors the user in their personal data.
  • Filter strip with icon-only pills + Refresh button. All 5 media-type pills (Movies, TV, Books, Games, Manga) fit without horizontal scrolling. Tap an icon to filter; tap again to deselect. No "All" pill needed — deselected state = all. The Refresh recommendations button lives on the right of the same strip, styled distinctly (teal background) so it reads as an action, not a filter. Compact intro section above makes room.
  • Refresh button placement matches what we discussed. Top of the page where it's reachable, but visually grouped with filters since both are "shape the recommendations" controls. Rate-limited per the caching investigation (5/day per user).
  • Each source row shows full context: source item thumbnail, "Because you rated" framing, source title in larger serif, your rating in gold stars. Visual hierarchy makes clear THIS source generated THESE connections.
  • Why descriptions stay the same as For You — one italic line characterizing what the connections share.
  • Connection cards larger here (110px wide) than in For You (105px) to give room for the extra meta line — media type + creator/year — since this page is the "deeper dive" view.
  • Library status indicator on connection cards. Small eye icon in a teal circle, top-right corner of the cover. Means "you've completed/seen this." Eye icon works metaphorically across all media types — you've seen the movie, read the book, played the game. Distinct from the bookmark icon used elsewhere for "want to" status. Cards you haven't engaged with show no indicator (discovery-worth).
  • "See all N connections →" per row opens a deeper view of just that source's connections (could be its own page or an expanded inline view).
  • "+ N more source items" loads progressively as user scrolls. No tap required — additional source rows fade in as the user reaches the bottom of the visible set. Less friction than a tap-to-expand, more controlled than dumping everything at once. The indicator visually shows there's more below the fold.
  • Serendipity section at bottom uses the muted gold border-left treatment we established. "Items beloved by the broader community, not tied to your specific source items" — honest framing.

What this page is NOT

  • Not a replacement for For You. This is the deep-dive into one specific section. For You stays the home.
  • Not Explore. Explore is for broad catalog browsing. This is for your personalized cross-media graph.
  • Not for items you haven't rated. Source items always come from items you've rated highly. If a user has zero highly-rated items, this page shows an onboarding state (rate 5 items to unlock cross-shelf).

Empty/onboarding state needed

  • User has zero highly-rated items → onboarding CTA ("Rate 5 items to start building your cross-shelf")
  • User has 1-4 highly-rated items → shows what's available + encouragement to rate more
  • User has filtered to a type with zero connections → "No book connections from your current ratings. Try rating more books, or remove the filter."
